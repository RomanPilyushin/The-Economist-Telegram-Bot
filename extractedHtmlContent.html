Argentina elected Javier Milei, a self-proclaimed “anarcho-capitalist”, as its president. Mr Milei defeated Sergio Massa, the economy minister, in a run-off. With counting nearly complete, Mr Milei had secured 56% of the vote. Inflation was a top concern for voters—the annual rate stood above 140% in October. Mr Milei campaigned on a radical economic platform that included scrapping the central bank, dollarising the economy and slashing public spending by 15% of GDP.

Satya Nadella, Microsoft’s chief executive, announced that Sam Altman, who was sacked as OpenAI’s boss on Friday, will join the firm to “lead a new advanced AI research team”. Speculation had swirled all weekend that, under pressure from investors and staff, OpenAI would reinstate Mr Altman. Instead, Mr Nadella also welcomed Emmett Shear, former head of Twitch, a video-streaming service, as OpenAI’s interim leader. Microsoft is OpenAI’s biggest investor.

Israeli tanks were reportedly closing in on the Indonesian hospital in northern Gaza. The enclave’s Hamas-run health ministry said there were some 700 people there; it claimed that 12 people were killed by artillery fire. Israel did not comment. Earlier, Israel released evidence it claims proves “Hamas is waging terror from hospitals,” showing apparent tunnels under al-Shifa hospital, Gaza’s largest facility.

Volodymyr Zelensky dismissed the commander of Ukraine’s medical forces and demanded swift changes to his country’s military operations. Ukraine’s president said soldiers needed a “fundamentally new level” of medical support after paramedics accused their boss, Tetiana Ostashchenko, of not securing enough first-aid kits. The changes come amid a faltering counter-offensive against Russia.

Iranian-backed Houthi rebels seized a cargo ship in the Red Sea, taking 25 crew members hostage. The rebels, who are based in Yemen, said they hijacked the vessel because of its links to Israel; last week the group warned that it considered all Israeli ships targets. Israel’s government insisted the vessel is British-owned and Japanese-operated. The ship’s owner is a firm registered in Britain but reportedly owned by an Israeli billionaire.

The chief executive of Optus, Australia’s second-biggest telecoms firm, resigned after an outage left nearly half the country without mobile or internet connectivity for more than 12 hours earlier this month. Kelly Bayer Rosmarin will be replaced by Michael Venter, the current CFO. Optus is also facing a lawsuit over a data breach that affected 10m Australians last year.

Australia beat India in the final of the Cricket World Cup in the Narendra Modi stadium in Ahmedabad. The 130,000 shocked spectators included the Indian prime minister, after whom the stadium is named. India had beaten Australia once already in this tournament. But the game that really mattered (and it really, really mattered) got away from them.
