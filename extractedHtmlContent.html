Israel reissued evacuation warnings to residents in southern Gaza as its army vowed to advance “wherever Hamas exists”. Hundreds of thousands of Palestinians have fled south to escape Israel’s assault on the northern part of the enclave. Following pressure from America, Israel agreed to let two trucks of diesel enter the Gaza Strip every day. The return of fuel would allow the resumption of UN aid deliveries and the restoration of mobile communications in Gaza.

Sam Altman was sacked as boss of OpenAI, the Microsoft-backed company behind ChatGPT. Mira Murati, the chief technology officer, will be his interim replacement. The firm’s board said it had lost confidence in Mr Altman because “he was not consistently candid in his communications”. Mr Altman co-founded OpenAI in 2015 with Greg Brockman, its chairman, who resigned in response to his sacking.

Several companies, including Apple, Disney and IBM, suspended advertising on X (formerly Twitter) amid concerns over antisemitism on the social-media platform. The boycott was triggered by Elon Musk touting an antisemitic conspiracy theory on Wednesday. Mr Musk later said his tweet only referred to certain “Jewish communities”. Since purchasing X last year, Mr Musk has loosened the platform’s moderation policies and gutted its safety team.

Australia’s defence minister, Richard Marles, criticised the “unsafe and unprofessional” conduct of a Chinese warship after an encounter with an Australian frigate, in which divers were injured. The incident occurred in Japan’s exclusive economic zone, as divers completed maintenance on the Australian ship. Mr Marles said that the Chinese vessel ignored requests to stay clear.

A meeting between two opposition parties in Taiwan failed to decide whose candidate should lead a joint ticket for the presidential election in January. The parties agreed to use opinion polls to choose between Hou Yu-ih of the Kuomintang and Ko Wen-je of the smaller Taiwan People’s Party, but squabbled over how to interpret them. Presidential candidates have until Friday to register.

George Weah, Liberia’s leader, conceded defeat to Joseph Boakai in a presidential election. Mr Boakai, the opposition leader, has secured an unassailable 51% of the vote with counting almost complete. Mr Weah’s six-year term has been marred by food insecurity and unemployment. But the former football star’s concession marks a rare democratic transfer of power in west Africa.

SpaceX is expected to make another attempt to launch its Starship spacecraft from its base on the Texas coast. The launch had been scheduled to take place on Friday but the rocketry firm’s founder, Elon Musk, said it had been postponed to Saturday in order to replace a “grid fin actuator”. An attempted launch in April failed after an explosion.
